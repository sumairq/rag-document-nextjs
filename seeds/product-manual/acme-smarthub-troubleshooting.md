# Acme SmartHub X1 — Troubleshooting

## The hub shows a solid red LED
A solid red LED means the hub has lost its network connection. Check that your
router is online, then power-cycle the hub by unplugging it for 10 seconds. If
red persists, the hub may be too far from the router; move it closer or add a
Wi-Fi extender.

## Accessories keep going offline
Intermittent accessory dropouts are usually caused by 2.4 GHz interference.
Keep the hub at least 1 meter away from microwaves, baby monitors, and USB 3.0
devices, which are known sources of interference. You can also change the hub's
Wi-Fi channel in the Acme Home app under Settings → Network → Channel.

## Firmware update is stuck
If the LED has been fast-blinking white for more than 15 minutes, the update
has likely stalled. Do not unplug the hub during a normal update, but if it has
exceeded 15 minutes, power-cycle it once. The hub will resume the update from
where it left off.

## Forgotten setup code
The 8-digit setup code is printed on the label on the bottom of the hub. If the
label is unreadable, perform a factory reset; a new code is not generated, but
the QR code can be re-scanned after reset.

## Warranty
The SmartHub X1 includes a 2-year limited warranty covering manufacturing
defects. Accidental damage and water damage are not covered. Contact Acme
support with your serial number (found under the QR code) to start a claim.
